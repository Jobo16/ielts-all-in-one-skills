## Model Essay
Some people believe that advertising is highly effective in persuading consumers to purchase products, while others argue that it has become so widespread that people tend to ignore it. This essay will discuss both views and argue that although excessive advertising reduces its overall impact, well-targeted and carefully designed advertisements remain highly influential.
On the one hand, the sheer volume of advertising in modern life has led to a degree of consumer fatigue. With the rise of the Internet, individuals are constantly exposed to advertisements on websites, social media platforms, and mobile applications. As a result, many people have become desensitised to such content and may even develop negative attitudes towards it. For instance, in countries like China, it is common for applications and websites to display multiple pop-up advertisements upon opening. Consequently, users often skip these advertisements immediately or install ad-blocking software to avoid them altogether. This suggests that excessive exposure can significantly reduce the effectiveness of advertising.
On the other hand, not all advertising is ignored, particularly when it is strategically tailored to individual consumers. Advances in data collection and algorithmic technology enable companies to analyse users’ online behaviour and deliver highly personalised advertisements. Such targeted content is more likely to capture attention and influence purchasing decisions. Furthermore, many companies enhance the appeal of their advertisements by using endorsements from celebrities and social media influencers, which can increase credibility and trust. As a result, consumers may be more inclined to engage with and respond to these advertisements.
In conclusion, while the overabundance of advertising has led many people to disregard it, this does not mean that advertising has lost its persuasive power entirely. In my view, well-designed and targeted advertising continues to play a significant role in shaping consumer behaviour, especially in the digital age.(297)
