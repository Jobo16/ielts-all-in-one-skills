Nowadays, many companies emphasise that their products are new or innovative in their advertisements. This essay will discuss the reasons behind this trend and explain why I believe it is largely a negative development.
One major reason businesses focus on promoting new features is the intense level of competition in modern markets. Since consumers are surrounded by a vast range of similar products, companies must find ways to distinguish themselves from their rivals. Advertising a product as “new” or “improved” can attract public attention more effectively and persuade consumers that the item is superior to older alternatives. As a result, businesses are more likely to increase sales and strengthen their market position. For example, smartphone manufacturers frequently introduce minor upgrades and market them aggressively in order to encourage customers to purchase the latest model.
However, I believe this trend has more negative than positive effects. Firstly, the constant pressure to release updated products can encourage companies to prioritise marketing over genuine quality improvement. In some cases, businesses exaggerate the benefits of new features simply to remain competitive, which may mislead consumers. Secondly, this form of advertising contributes to a culture of excessive consumerism. Many people feel pressured to replace products that are still functional because advertisements create the impression that older versions are outdated or inferior. This not only causes financial stress for consumers but also increases environmental waste due to unnecessary disposal of goods.
