Many people argue that professionals, such as engineers and doctors, should be required to work in the country where they completed their training. However, others believe that it is their right to work in another country. This essay will discuss both ideas and argue that professionals should have the freedom to choose where to work.
Staying in the country where they were trained can help ease the local shortage of professionals. Many countries nowadays face a shortage of experts, especially in industries like engineering, medicine and infrastructure construction.
As a result, requiring experts to work locally after their training is a reasonable and effective way to meet local needs. In addition, whether the experts are coming from another country or not, the country where they were trained needs to spend numerous resources to support their training. Thus, staying in that country for some time after their training gives these professionals a chance to give back to local communities.
Therefore, requiring professionals to work locally after their training is a practical way to address these needs. Moreover, training professionals involves significant investment by the country, and requiring them to stay for a period allows them to contribute back to their local communities.
In terms of allowing those professionals to work in another country, working abroad may have higher salary and better treatment. For those professions, such as doctors and engineers, people are being paid higher in developed countries statistically. Consequently, those who pursue financial rewards may choose to work abroad instead of their home countries. For the international students, many of them went abroad due to better education and more advanced technology. However, they may want to use the knowledge they have learned to come back home later and contribute to society.
On the other hand, working abroad can offer higher salaries and better benefits. Professionals such as doctors and engineers often earn more in developed countries, which motivates some to seek employment overseas. Many international students also study abroad to access superior education and advanced technology, and some may later choose to return home to apply their skills and contribute to society.

Many people argue that professionals, such as doctors and engineers, should be obliged to work in the country where they received their training. Others, however, believe they should have the right to work abroad. This essay will examine both perspectives and argue that professionals should ultimately be free to decide where to work.
Remaining in the country of training can help alleviate local shortages of skilled professionals. Many nations face deficits in critical sectors such as engineering, medicine, and infrastructure development. Requiring professionals to work locally after completing their education is a practical way to address these needs. Furthermore, training professionals involves substantial investment by the state, and staying in the country allows them to contribute back to the communities that supported their development.
