## Model Answer
The line graph illustrates the number of tourists visiting a Caribbean island between 2010 and 2017, measured in millions, and distinguishes between visitors staying on cruise ships and those staying on the island.
Overall, the total number of tourists increased substantially over the period. While visitors staying on the island initially made up the majority, the number of cruise ship tourists rose rapidly and eventually overtook them. In contrast, the figure for those staying on the island grew more moderately before leveling off.
In 2010, approximately 0.75 million tourists stayed on the island, compared to only 0.25 million cruise ship visitors, giving a total of around 1 million. The number of visitors staying on the island increased steadily to about 1.5 million by 2013, after which it remained relatively stable, with a slight dip in 2016 before recovering in the final year.
By comparison, the number of tourists staying on cruise ships remained low and even declined slightly in 2012, falling to around 0.25 million. However, from 2013 onwards, this figure rose sharply, reaching 1 million in 2014 and continuing to climb to 2 million by 2017. Notably, cruise ship visitors surpassed those staying on the island in 2016. (184)
