## Feedback
1. Eliminate basic errors
Articles (the, a) / Plurals
2. Improve academic tone
Replace: “a lot / drastic / suffered”With: significant / steady / gradual / declined
3. Use data-driven language
Always prefer: “rose from X to Y” / “declined to” / “remained stable at”
4. Avoid repetition
Instead of repeating: “percentage of urban population”
Use: the proportion / the figure / the share
5. Control sentence structure
Avoid very long sentences
Keep grammar clean and consistent

Sample
The line graph illustrates the proportion of people living in urban areas in four Asian countries between 1970 and 2020, with projections extending to 2040.
Overall, the percentage of urban residents increased in all four countries over the period. Malaysia consistently recorded the highest figures, while Indonesia experienced the most rapid growth. By contrast, Thailand remained the least urbanised country despite a steady upward trend.
In 1970, Indonesia had the lowest proportion of urban population, at approximately 14%. However, this figure rose steadily over the following decades, reaching just over 50% in 2020 and is expected to climb further to around 60% by 2040. Similarly, Malaysia saw a substantial increase, with its urban population rising from about 30% in 1970 to nearly 75% in 2020. It is projected to maintain its leading position, exceeding 80% by the end of the period.
The Philippines showed a more fluctuating pattern. After remaining stable at around 30% between 1970 and 1980, the proportion increased to roughly 50% in 1990 before declining gradually over the next two decades. It then recovered and is forecast to rise steadily in the future. In contrast, Thailand experienced a consistent but moderate increase throughout the period, growing from below 20% in 1970 to around 30% in 2020, and is expected to accelerate slightly, although it will remain the lowest among the four countries.
