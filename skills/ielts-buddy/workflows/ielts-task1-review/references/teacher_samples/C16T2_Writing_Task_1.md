In the next stage, the juice passes through a limestone filter to remove impurities. The purified liquid is then heated in an evaporator, causing it to thicken into syrup. Following this, the syrup is placed in a centrifuge, which separates the sugar crystals from the remaining liquid. Finally, the crystals undergo drying and cooling processes, resulting in the finished sugar product.(182)
