The diagram illustrates the process of manufacturing instant noodles, from the initial storage of flour to the final packaged product ready for sale.
Overall, the production involves eight stages, which can be broadly divided into two phases: noodle production and packaging. Flour is first processed into dough, which is then shaped into noodle discs, before being fried and dried. In the final stages, the dried noodle cakes are packed into cups with additional ingredients and prepared for distribution.
At the beginning of the process, flour is delivered to storage silos and subsequently transferred to a mixer, where it is combined with water and oil. The resulting dough is then passed through a series of rollers to form thin sheets. These sheets are cut into strips and shaped into circular noodle discs. Following this, the discs are fried in oil and then dried to remove excess moisture.
In the packaging stage, the dried noodle cakes are placed into individual cups, to which vegetables and spices are added. Finally, the filled cups are sealed and labelled, producing finished instant noodle products ready for sale.(182)
